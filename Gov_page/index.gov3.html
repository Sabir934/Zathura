IBPS PO Recruitment 2025


Probationary Officers (PO) and Management Trainees (MT) are hired annually via a Common Recruitment Process (CRP), more popularly referred to as the IBPS PO exam. The CRP began in 2011 and is currently running for the 15th time this year so is properly referred to as IBPS PO /MT CRP XV 2025. The selection process for IBPS PO has 3 stages (preliminary, mains and interview) and the candidate needs to clear each one of them in order to go to the next stage. Below are the significant steps of the IBPS PO Recruitment Process:


    Online Preliminary Examination
  • A sufficient number of candidates in each category are shortlisted by IBPS as per requirements for Online Main Examination

    • Online Mains Examination

    Candidates need to clear the sectional as well as an overall cutoff in the main exam to be invited for an interview.


    Interview

    The interview will be carried out by the Participating Organizations and coordinated by the Nodal Bank in each State/ UT with the assistance of IBPS


    Final Result

    Final result **is announced** on the basis of candidates' overall performance in IBPS PO Mains and Interview Rounds.


    Provisional Allotment

    The provisional allocation of the selected candidate is announced on the official website. The IBPS Banks are allocated as per candidates' preference which is given during online application.


    BPS does its share in the selection process up to the provisional allotment to various banks only. Once the provisional allotment is made to the various banks, it becomes the job of the concerned banks to intimate the selected candidates for their joining date and procedures.     IBPS PO 2025 Exam Pattern

    IBPS provided individual sectional timing for every section in the preliminary exam. The IBPS PO exam (CRP XIII) for the recruitment of candidates will be conducted through an online written test in two parts: Tier 1 or IBPS Preliminary Exam and Tier 2 or IBPS Mains Exam. This is followed by an interview process through face-to-face interviews.

    IBPS PO Prelims Exam Pattern 2025


    The IBPS PO pre-exam is 20 minutes long for each of the sections and will be held online. It comprises 3 sections with 100 questions and a maximum of 100 marks. In the IBPS PO pre-exam, there is a negative marking and 0.25 marks are reduced for every incorrect answer. The candidates need to clear the cut-off in all 3 sections to be eligible for the IBPS PO Main exam. The section-wise information is provided below:


    IBPS PO Prelims Exam Pattern 2025

    SR.NO Name of Tests(Objetive) No.of Questions Maximum Marks Duration
    1. English Language 30 30 20 minutes
    2 Numerical Ability 35 35 20 minutes
    3. Reasoning Ability 35 35 20 Minutes
    Total --- 100 100 60 Minutes

    The candidates need to qualify in all of the three tests by achieving minimum cut-off marks to be determined by IBPS. The sufficient number of candidates in every class as determined by IBPS as per requirement will be shortlisted for Online Main Examination.


    IBPS PO Mains Exam Pattern 2025


    IBPO PO Main test is also an online test, it consists of 5 (4+1) sections worth a total score of 200+25 marks and a total time of 180+30 minutes. IBPS will organize a descriptive paper in its mains test for choosing candidates for the position of Probationary Officer. The sections should be attempted in the same sequence and the time assigned as required at the time of the test. A candidate has to pass the preliminary and mains examinations to appear for the interview process.

    Penalty for Incorrect Responses: There is a penalty for incorrect responses filled in the Objective Tests of Preliminary as well as Mains exams. An incorrect response given by the candidate to an objective type question will result in a penalty of one-fourth of the marks allotted to the question. For instance, 0.25 marks shall be deducted for an incorrect response to a question that is of 1 mark. There is no penalty for not answering a question, i.e. if the candidate doesn't mark any answer.


    IBPS PO Interview


    The candidates shortlisted for mains exam are invited for a face to face interview, which is of a maximum of 100 marks. The minimum pass mark in the interview is 40% (35% in case of SC/ST/OBC/PWD candidates).


    The interview session is typically 15-20 minutes in duration, during which a panel of bank authorities interviews the candidates on themselves, banking industry, general awareness, current affairs, etc. Candidates should be smartly dressed, confident and take all necessary documents to the interview.


    Final merit list is made based on marks secured in main examination and interview. Final marks are computed by IBPS assigning weightage to marks awarded in main examination and interview in the ratio of 80:20 respectively.


    Final Score Calculation for IBPS PO Exam
